Biography

Full professor in Mechatronics Systems at UEVE and Paris-Saclay University. From 2011 to 2019, I was an associate professor in humanoid robotics at UVSQ, holding at the same time an industrial excellence chair about hydraulic domestication at UVSQ & BIA company. From 2010 to 2011, I was a Post-doc researcher at TUM-Germany with an Alexander Von Humboldt Foundation scholarship. I received my Ph.D. in robotics from UVSQ in 2009. I was awarded the best Ph.D. thesis in robotics by the French CNRS in 2010 and the best Ph.D. thesis in mechatronics for the 20th anniversary of UVSQ. I have been an investigator of several French national projects. I am the leader of the mechatronics research group. I was the team leader of the Paris-Saclay team for Mohamed Bin Zayed International Robotics Challenge MBZIRC2017. I am the Institute for Control and Decision (iCode Paris-Saclay) co-coordinator of the Human-in-the-loop Control and Decision Institute (H-CoDe Paris-Saclay). I published 28 peer-reviewed original publications and 51 international and national patents. He obtained 2.5 M€ funding during the last five years.

As A Mechatronics Design & Robotics Expert

...My goals are to help disabled people walk by building strong, powerful and safe robotic systems. Their behavior should be able to reach human beings' performances and improve their autonomy. Yet this will also push application's domains around Healthcare and Silver Economy by touching aged and disabled people.
